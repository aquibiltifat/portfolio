A Comprehensive Review of the Fatwa on Terrorism and Suicide Bombings by Dr. Muhammad Tahir-ul-Qadri

Dr. Muhammad Tahir-ul-Qadri
4.7/5

A Comprehensive Review of the Fatwa on Terrorism and Suicide Bombings analyzes Dr. Muhammad Tahir-ul-Qadri’s detailed religious decree condemning all forms of terrorism, extremism, and suicide attacks as un-Islamic. The review highlights how Dr. Qadri uses classical Islamic jurisprudence, Quranic verses, and Hadith to refute the ideological justifications used by extremist groups. It explores the methodological rigor of his argument, showing that he distinguishes between legitimate jihad and unlawful violence against innocents. The review also examines the fatwa’s global impact, including its reception among scholars, governments, and interfaith communities, as well as its role in promoting peace, tolerance, and the true spirit of Islam in the 21st century.

Approximately 475–500 pages, depending on the edition Originally written in Urdu; later translated into English and Arabic 2010
